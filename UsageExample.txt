Here’s a short, user-focused guide that explains how to operate the app (keyboard + mouse) and what to expect to see on screen.

Quick start
Launch the app (the game window opens).
You’ll see a 2D grid of cells. Particles are rendered as small textured squares on the grid.
Use the mouse and keyboard (below) to paint particles, move the view, and zoom.
Mouse
Left click (or hold left button): paint particles at the cursor position using the current brush size and the currently selected particle type.
Holding the left button continuously paints while you move the mouse.
The cursor position is mapped to a grid cell — click a cell to add particles there (the app converts your screen position into grid coordinates automatically).
Keyboard
Number keys — change particle type:
1 = Sand
2 = Water
3 = Smoke
4 = Stone
0 = Erase (Empty particle)
(Only 0–4 are mapped; other digits are ignored by default.)
Space bar — “spray” a larger burst at the cursor (uses a bigger brush so you get a stronger/faster fill).
Arrow keys — pan the camera/view:
Left / Right — move view horizontally.
Up / Down — move view vertically.
. (period) and / (slash) — zoom the camera forward/backward.
Period (.) zooms one direction (zoom in), Slash (/) zooms the opposite (zoom out).
Esc — close the window / exit the app.
What each particle type does (expected on-screen behavior)
Sand (1)
Falls downward under gravity.
Piles up and stacks when it hits other solids.
Can fall diagonally around obstacles and through empty space.
Water (2)
Falls and then spreads/flows horizontally.
Moves into empty spaces and disperses according to simple fluid rules.
Smoke (3)
Behaves like a gas — tends to move upward and spread out.
Stone (4)
Immovable solid — does not fall or move; acts as a barrier.
Empty (0)
Removes particles leaving an empty cell.
Visual cues to watch for

Textured squares represent particle types (each type uses a different sprite/texture).
Grid lines are shown or hidden depending on zoom level — when zoomed in, the cell grid will be visible; when zoomed out it may hide to reduce clutter.
When you paint, you’ll see immediate placement of textured tiles in a square brush shape centered on the cursor.
Dynamic motion happens after particles are placed — you should see sand fall and pile, water flow, and smoke rise.
Practical examples

Painting a sand dune:
Select key 1 (Sand).
Hold left mouse and sweep near the top of the grid; sand will fall and pile below.
Making a pond:
Select key 2 (Water).
Press space while hovering over a few grid cells to add a larger amount of water at once; watch it spread.
Building a barrier:
Select key 4 (Stone) and click a line to block falling particles.
Tips & expected limitations

If you hold the left mouse button, the app will continuously add particles, which can rapidly fill an area.
Keep an eye on zoom: if you’re too far out you may not be able to place single-cell precision; zoom in to paint fine details.
Performance: painting many particles quickly may reduce frame rate on slower machines — consider smaller brushes if the display becomes sluggish.
If you accidentally fill an area, press 0 (Empty) and paint to clear cells.
Exit

Press Esc or close the window through the window frame to exit the application.
